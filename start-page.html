Welcome to the CONSPIR Survey

Thank you for taking part in this research study. This survey is part of the CONSPIR project — an academic initiative at TU Darmstadt that investigates how people express and recognize linguistic markers of conspiracy thinking across different online domains.

What is CONSPIR?

The CONSPIR framework is designed to identify and analyze patterns of language that commonly appear in conspiracy-related communication. It combines computational linguistics and social science insights to better understand how conspiracy narratives are formed, spread, and perceived.

As part of this research, we are developing systems that can automatically detect certain conspiratorial traits (such as excessive suspicion, contradictory reasoning, or claims of hidden intent) in online text.

Purpose of This Survey

The goal of this survey is to evaluate how well our system aligns with human intuition. By comparing your judgments with automated predictions, we can refine our models and improve the accuracy and fairness of linguistic detection systems.

Your responses will help us understand which language features humans perceive as conspiratorial — and how these perceptions vary across different topics and contexts.

How the Survey Works

You will be shown a series of short online posts (e.g., tweets or comments). For each post, you will be asked to:

  • Indicate whether the post shows certain conspiratorial traits
  • Rate your confidence in each judgment
  • Optionally, provide feedback if something seems unclear or ambiguous

The survey takes approximately 10–15 minutes to complete. There are no right or wrong answers — we are interested in your perspective as a reader.

Data and Privacy

Your responses are collected anonymously and used solely for academic research. No personally identifiable information is stored or shared. You may withdraw from the survey at any time without any consequence.

If you have any questions about the study, please contact the research team at research@conspir-project.org.
