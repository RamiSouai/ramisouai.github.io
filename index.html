What is CONSPIR?

CONSPIR is an acronym for "the seven traits of conspiratorial thinking", they are:

  • Contradictory: Holding inconsistent or self-contradictory beliefs without recognizing the contradiction.
    Example: "I don't trust mainstream media because it's always lying — but I only read headlines from that same network."
  • Overriding suspicion: Excessive distrust of official sources, experts, or mainstream explanations.
    Example: "They say the vaccine is safe, but it's obviously a cover-up — never trust the 'experts'.
  • Nefarious intent: Assuming malicious motives behind events, actions, or policies without evidence.
    Example: "The city installed new streetlights — obviously they're tracking us to control behavior."
  • Persecuted victim: Believing oneself or one's group is being unfairly targeted or oppressed.
    Example: "Everyone's out to get our community — they're banning our traditions on purpose."
  • Immune to evidence: Dismissing or reinterpreting contradictory evidence to maintain existing beliefs.
    Example: "Even though multiple studies show otherwise, those results were paid for by corrupt interests."
  • Re-interpreting randomness: Seeing patterns, connections, or meaning in random or coincidental events.
    Example: "Three celebrities tweeted the same phrase — it's proof they're coordinating a secret message."

The CONSPIR Plug-in

Using the CONSPIR traits as a framework, we developped a broswer extension that examines online text-content, such as social media posts,articles, or comments, and scans for the presence of CONSPIR traits. The Plug-in's primary goal is to forewarn users about the presence of language indicative of conspiratorial thinking, before that content creates a conspiratorial belief. By highlighting the presence of CONSPIR traits, the extension aims to encourage more critical evaluation of the information being consumed.

Purpose of this survey

This survey aims to measure the Plug-in's usefulness and usabilitymeasure the real-world usefulness and usability of the CONSPIR Plug-in. Your participation will help us answer critical research questions, including:

  • How accurate is the Plug-in in detecting CONSPIR traits?
  • Does plug-in invite the user to think critically about the content they're consuming?
  • How usable is the Plug-in?

How the Survey Works

This survey is structured to allow us to compare your judgment both with and without the aid of our tool. You will be presented with a series of online posts. For each post, you will be shown the content:

  • Once without the CONSPIR Plug-in activated.
  • Once with the CONSPIR Plug-in activated.

For each instance, you will be asked to:

  • Indicate whether the post shows certain conspiratorial traits
  • Rate your confidence in each judgment
  • Optionally, provide feedback if something seems unclear or ambiguous

Please note that the first 3 questions are dedicated practice runs, to familiarize yourself with the Plug-in's interface and the rating process before starting the main section.
