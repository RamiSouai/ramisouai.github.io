What is CONSPIR?

CONSPIR CONSPIR is an acronym representing the seven traits of conspiratorial thinking. These traits describe common patterns found in conspiracy-oriented reasoning:

  • Contradictory: Holding inconsistent or self-contradictory beliefs without recognizing the contradiction.
    Example: "The ice-caps are not melting! And the rise in temperature is not caused-by human activity!"
  • Overriding suspicion: Excessive distrust of official sources, experts, or mainstream explanations.
    Example: "They say the vaccine is safe, but it's obviously a cover-up!'.
  • Nefarious intent: Assuming malicious motives behind events, actions, or policies without evidence.
    Example: "Mainstream media only serves the interests of the elites!"
  • Persecuted victim: Believing oneself or one's group is being unfairly targeted or oppressed.
    Example: "Our country is being invaded by immigrants!"
  • Immune to evidence: Dismissing or reinterpreting contradictory evidence to maintain existing beliefs.
    Example: "The footage of the moonlanding was faked and filmed in a Hollywood basement!"
  • Re-interpreting randomness: Seeing patterns, connections, or meaning in random or coincidental events.
    Example: "These 5G towers gave me COVID!"

The CONSPIR Plug-in

Using the CONSPIR traits as a framework, we developped a broswer extension that examines online text-content, such as social media posts, articles, or comments, and scans for the presence of CONSPIR traits. The Plug-in's primary goal is to forewarn users about the presence of language indicative of conspiratorial thinking, before that content creates a conspiratorial belief. By highlighting the presence of CONSPIR traits, the extension aims to encourage more critical evaluation of the information being consumed.

Purpose of this survey

The purpose of this survey is to evaluate the usefulness and practicality of the CONSPIR Plug-in, as well as its ability to motivate users to think more critically about online content.

How the Survey Works

The survey is designed to compare your judgments with and without the assistance of the CONSPIR Plug-in. You will be shown a series of online posts and asked whether you think each contains conspiratorial content. The posts will alternate between rounds with the plug-in activated and without it.

You will see a ✅ icon if it is a round where the plug-in is disabled.

The survey is structured in two rounds. In the first round, you will be shown twitter posts, on rounds where the plug-in is activated, you will see a ⚠️ icon. Clicking on it will reveal the detected conspir traits.

In the second round, long-form text will be shown (Reddit posts and articles). The Plug-in will analyse the sentences and highlight those with possible CONSPIR traits.

For each question, you will be asked to:

  • Indicate whether or not you think the post shows certain conspiratorial traits
  • Rate your confidence in your judgment
  • Optionally, provide feedback if something seems unclear or ambiguous or if you have any comments or questions

You may begin with a practice round to familiarize yourself with the plug-in or proceed directly to the main survey.

Additional Information

  • You do not need to download the browser extension. For the purposes of this survey, the plug-in’s functionality is already embedded in this website.
  • The survey takes approximately 30 minutes to complete.
  • There are no right or wrong answers.
